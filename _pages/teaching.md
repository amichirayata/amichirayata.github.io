I am passionate about teaching and mentoring, and I strongly believe in giving back to the learning community. During my Master's studies at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), I served as a Teaching Assistant (TA) for two graduate-level courses offered by the Department of Electrical Engineering. These courses provided me with valuable experience in conducting tutorials, grading assignments and examinations, mentoring students, and helping bridge the gap between theory and practical understanding.

### [E1 244: Detection and Estimation Theory](https://cps.iisc.ac.in/faculty/vaibhav/E12442025.html)

**Graduate-level course, Indian Institute of Science**

* **Instructor:** Prof. Vaibhav Katewa 
* **Student Strength:** ~80 graduate students
* **Topics Covered:** Statistical signal processing, hypothesis testing, parameter estimation, Bayesian estimation, maximum likelihood methods, Cramér–Rao bounds, and detection theory.
* **Responsibilities:** Conducted tutorials, prepared and evaluated assignments, assisted with examinations, and provided academic support to students throughout the semester.

### [E2 202: Random Processes](https://ece.iisc.ac.in/~rajeshs/E2202.html)

**Graduate-level course, Indian Institute of Science**

* **Instructor:** Prof. Anurag Kumar and Prof. Rajesh Sundaresan
* **Student Strength:** ~100 graduate and senior undergraduate students
* **Topics Covered:** Probability theory, stochastic processes, random variables, correlation analysis, spectral representations, Markov processes, and applications in communication and signal processing.
* **Responsibilities:** Assisted in tutorials and problem-solving sessions, graded assignments and examinations, and mentored students on course projects and coursework.

Teaching these courses strengthened my appreciation for the mathematical foundations of signal processing and machine learning, while also helping me develop effective communication and mentoring skills.
